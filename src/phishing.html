What is Phishing?

Phishing is a cybersecurity attack that is used to steal user data or gain network access. Phishing attacks often start off with a fraudulent email or other communication such as a text message or phone call to lure a victim to give out confidential information. The message is made to look as if it came from a trusted sender to trick the victim. Phishing messages frequently use emotions like fear, urgency, greed, and curiosity to entice readers to open attachments or click links.


Types of Phishing

Spear Phishing is a targeted attack at a specific person (picking a specific fish).
Whaling is a targeted attack at executives of a company such as the CEO, CFO, or any Cxx.
Smishing is an attack that manipulates victims over text messages/SMS (short message service) to give out sensitive information.
Vishing is an attack that manipulates victims to give out sensitive information over the phone.
Search engine phishing is an attack that entices victims to visit their website on a search engine. Although the process to search a website may be legitimate, the website manipulates users to give out personal information.

Email phishing is the most common type of phishing attack that starts with a fraudulent email that entices victims to provide confidential information or may contain dangerous links.


Common Email Phishing Techniques

  • Contains an unknown sender.
  • The email is poorly written.
  • The email has little content.
  • There is little-to-no credibility.
  • The email contains altered brand logos.
  • The message creates a sense of urgency or feels threatening.
  • The message is "too good to be true."
  • The email includes suspicious attachments or links.

Know Your URL

We encounter an abundance of URLs daily. It is a good idea to become aware of suspicious URLs. If you are not sure, use a URL checker like Virus Total. Below are some common techniques for suspicious URLs.

  1. Starts with a legit site, but actually sends you to Russia.
    • http://www.amazon.com.cust_login.ru/
  2. Ends with a legit site, but actually sends you to Czech Republic.
    • http://accounts_login.cz/www.amazon.com
  3. IP Address (cannot tell where you are going).
    • http://65.26.114.84/cust_login.html
  4. Shortened URLs (cannot tell where you are going).
    • http://tinyurl.com/gq854qg
  5. Variations on legit domains (visit at your own risk)!
    • http://www.amazonn.com/
    • http://www.amazon.co/
    • http://www.ofice.com/
