What Is Cybersecurity?

Cyber security refers to the practices, processes, and technologies designed to protect systems, networks, and programs from digital attacks. These attacks can impact a variety of systems from business servers to your personal phone. This topic is often referred to as information technology security due to its domain encompassing anything connected to the Internet of Things or IoT(IoT link here).

A Brief History of Malware

Since the release of the first personal computer in 1974, the Altair, digital threats have existed in the background. The term many people are familiar with “bug” was coined when computer scientist Grace hopper found the cause of an early computer malfunction to be a moth trapped in one of the relays. The first digital self-replicating virus was released in 1971 just 3 years before the first personal computer became available. This virus was designed to duplicate itself many times over until the hard drive became full and the computer unusable. Since then the rate at which digital threats have been evolving has seen the same curve as the evolution of computers themselves. In 1998 there was a virus that infected over 60 million computers worldwide causing an estimated billion dollars in damage known as the CIH computer virus. In that same year the first DDoS attacks ever recorded were attacks on both the Mexican government and the Pentagon. These viruses were some of many whose only goal was to cause disruption and slow down networks.

These threats evolved into a new level of malware in the years that followed with MYDOOM (2004) and the introduction of Scamware (2005). These had more refined goals than their predecessors allowing them to do more than just attack systems. MYDOOM was Malware that was spread through spam mail. This started by stealing the email addresses of those who fell victim allowing it to spread to more people, then it created a back door on the users computer adding it to a network of controlled computers known as a botnet(insert link to the other page here). Scamware operated with a similar goal but was spread through pop-ups warning the user that their computer may be infected then prompting them to download something. This allowed them to install malicious code that could do a number of different attacks on the hosts computer.

Not long after the aforementioned malware was released into the public new forms emerged which took cyber threats to the next level. These new forms of malware were designed to break into secure networks and steal entire databases of user data. The Zeus trojan in 2007 read the keys that users typed in order to steal credentials. It is most famous for stealing information from the department of transportation, but got into over 74,000 sites including BankofAmerica.com, NASA.gov, ABC.com, and Amazon.com. Other attempts used flaws in widely used software to exploit users. Conficker is an example of this which used a vulnerability in the Windows operating system to gain access to millions of computers since 2008 in its original release creating its own botnet from infected computers allowing it to carry out other malicious attacks such as data theft through phishing and brute force attacks as well as DDoS.

While there are many defenses out there for the known forms of malware, each time a bug is stomped out another few come to take its place. Each of the new threats is more complicated than the last taking account to what worked and didn’t from its predecessors. As time progresses the rate at which new viruses and worms appears increases exponentially. Since 2010 there have been too many new viruses for them to be easily listed, but a few of them include Stuxnet, Flame, CryptoLocker, Gameover Zeus, BASHLITE, WannaCry, and more recently Thanatos and Titanium. The new tactic in recent digital attacks has been focused on the increased use of keys and certificates that grant the user access to secure files and information within certain servers. One of the most famous examples of this is the Snowden compromise of 2013 in which Edward Snowden used fabricated keys to increase his permissions level and access the sensitive information in the NSA allowing him to leak it. Of all the forms of security Keys and Certificates are among the least likely to be detected and are extremely slow to resolve.

As we continue to become more and more dependent on information technology in every aspect of our lives we can only make guesses as to how these threats will adapt. The importance of cybersecurity comes into play when we try to stay one step ahead of any threat that may come our way. It will undoubtable continue to grow in importance as businesses and people need the protections and reassurances that come with further study in cybersecurity.
