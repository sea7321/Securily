How do I Protect Myself?

1. Train Your Staff

Training your staff to recognize cyber attacks is a one of the most efficient ways to protect your company and your employees. One of the most common techniques cyber criminals use against companies is phishing. For example, this may include fraudulent emails impersonating someone in the same organization are often used to obtain confidential information or certain access to company files/network.

2. Keep Software and Systems Up to Date

Cyber attacks often occur when software or systems are not up to date. Not being fully up to date may leave weaknesses for cybercriminals, so that they can use known exploits, or flaws, to target your system. In return, they may gain access to your network, so make sure to keep software up to date.

3. Install a Firewall

Installing a firewall is another step to make your network more secure. A firewall monitors incoming and outgoing traffic and decides to allow or block data packets based on a pre-defined set of network security rules.

4. Encrypt Data

Encryption is the process of converting information into code to prevent unauthorized access. If your data does fall into the wrong hands, there is an extra layer of security before your data becomes vulnerable.

5. Backup Your Data

Worst case scenario, to avoid loss of data, downtime, and financial loss, backing up your data is essential.

6. Wifi Security

When on a public network like in a cafe, library, or airport, pay special attention. Using a virtual private network (VPN) will allow you to create a secure network connection by encrypting your data over the network.

7. Passwords

With the overwhelming number of passwords that you need to keep track today, it is very difficult to remember all of your passwords. Do not fall into the trap of using the same password for multiple accounts. If your password is discovered, all of your accounts are now vulnerable. Instead, try a password vault that will allow you to auto-fill forms and logins. What is a strong password? Visit The Software Pro to see the Top 10 Password Mistakes.

8. Multi-Factor Authentication

Although having multi-factor authentication may be frustrating at times, it is another great way to add an extra layer(s) of security. Multi-factor authentication grants access to a website or application only after receiving two or more pieces of evidence, which includes knowledge, possession, and identity.

9. Install Anti-Malware & Antivirus Software

Malware detection is crucial to prevent cyber attacks as well as it serves as an early warning system regarding your computer's security. Antivirus software typically deals with older, more established threats like Trojans, viruses, and worms. However, anti-malware proactively detects and removes suspicious activity that it might not have ever seen before. As a result, it is best to have both kinds of software, but be cautious as there is fake antivirus/anti-malware software. Visit Tom's Guide to find out what software is right for you.
